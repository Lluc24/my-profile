## Visiting Student
- Programming Methods Lab, EPFL · Internship
- Sep 2025 - Jan 2026 · 5 mos
- Lausanne, Vaud, Switzerland · On-site
### Description
During my exchange at EPFL, I completed a 12 ECTS research project at the Laboratoire de Méthodes de Programmation (LAMP) under the direction of Prof. Martin Odersky (the creator of Scala and author of javac) and Matthieu Bovel. My research addressed a fundamental stability flaw in the Scala 3 compiler: the non-deterministic divergence of recursive Match Types. Previously, the compiler relied on catching JVM StackOverflowError to handle infinite type reductions, a practice that is fundamentally unsafe due to potential leaked locks and bypassed cleanup blocks. I designed and implemented a static divergence checking algorithm to replace this runtime detection with a formal termination guarantee.

The project culminated in PR #24661 (https://github.com/scala/scala3/pull/24661), where I introduced a mechanism that tracks type-reduction history and identifies "domination" through complexity vectors (structural AST node counts) and covering set analysis. By applying a formal termination proof based on a generalized Dickson’s Lemma, the algorithm ensures the type-checker always terminates while remaining flexible enough for complex value-level iterations like Peano arithmetic. My work includes detailed diagnostic reporting and extensive compiler safety improvements; it was awarded the maximum grade of 6.0/6.0.

### Keywords
